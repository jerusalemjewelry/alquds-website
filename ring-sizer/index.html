Sizing Guidelines & Reference

The Paper Strip / String Method

If you don't have a ring to measure, use this simple method at home:

  1. Cut a thin strip of paper or a piece of non-stretching string about 6 inches long.
  2. Wrap it snugly around the base of the finger you want to measure. Make sure it can slide over your knuckle!
  3. Use a pen to mark the exact point where the paper or string overlaps.
  4. Lay it flat and measure the length from the end to your pen mark with a ruler in millimeters.
  5. This measurement is your finger's circumference. Look it up in the chart to find your US size.
Pro Sizing Tips

Measure your fingers at the end of the day when they are warm. Do not measure when cold, as fingers can be half a size smaller. If you are between sizes, we always recommend ordering the larger size.

2. Cut-Out Finger Ring Sizer

  1. Carefully cut out the ring sizer strap below.
  2. Make a small vertical cut along the dotted line for Slot B.
  3. With the numbers facing outward, wrap the strap around the base of the finger to be measured.
  4. Pull the pointed end Tab A through Slot B until the paper wraps snugly around your finger.
  5. The line aligned with Slot B is your correct US ring size. (Read size at the top of the finger).
